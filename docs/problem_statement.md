# 🚧 Problem Statement: Retroreflectivity Measurement on National Highways

## 📌 Background

Road safety on highways heavily depends on the **visibility of traffic signs and pavement markings**, especially during **night-time and low-light conditions**. Retroreflectivity is the property that allows these surfaces to reflect light from vehicle headlights back toward the driver, ensuring clear visibility.

Over time, this retroreflective quality degrades due to:

* Continuous vehicular wear and tear
* Environmental factors such as dust, pollution, rain, and sunlight
* Aging of materials used in road markings and signboards

This degradation leads to reduced visibility, increasing the risk of accidents.

---

## ⚠️ Existing Challenges

### 1. Manual Measurement Limitations

Currently, retroreflectivity is measured using **handheld retroreflectometers**. This approach has several drawbacks:

* Time-consuming and inefficient for large highway networks
* Requires personnel to operate on active highways, posing safety risks
* Limited coverage due to manual operation

---

### 2. Lack of Continuous Monitoring

* Measurements are performed periodically rather than continuously
* Degradation between inspection cycles often goes unnoticed
* No real-time visibility into the condition of road assets

---

### 3. Scalability Issues

India’s national highway network spans thousands of kilometers, including:

* Multi-lane expressways
* Urban highways
* Rural road segments

Manual inspection methods cannot scale efficiently across such a vast network.

---

### 4. Environmental Variability

Retroreflectivity is affected by real-world conditions such as:

* Night vs day lighting
* Wet vs dry surfaces
* Fog, dust, and glare

Current methods do not effectively account for these dynamic conditions during measurement.

---

## 🎯 Core Problem

There is a need for a **safe, efficient, and scalable system** to measure and monitor the retroreflectivity of:

* Road markings
* Traffic signs
* Road studs and delineators

across diverse environmental and operational conditions, without relying on slow and unsafe manual processes.

---

## 🧠 Objective

To identify or develop a **technology-driven approach** that enables:

* Faster data collection
* Reduced human risk during measurement
* Coverage across large highway networks
* Reliable assessment under varying real-world conditions

---

## 📍 Expected Impact

Solving this problem will:

* Improve night-time driving safety
* Enable timely maintenance of road infrastructure
* Reduce accident risks due to poor visibility
* Support data-driven decision-making for highway authorities
